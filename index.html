About Me

Hello! I’m a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate in Astrophysics at the MIT Kavli Institute. I’m interested understanding the galaxy formation process and the dark matter density profiles of galaxies.

At MIT, I’m working with Prof. Lina Necib on various projects:

  • Applying graph neural network on infer the dark matter density profiles of dwarf galaxies from their stellar kinematics
  • Using kinematics of accreted stars to characterize the galaxy accretion history of the Milky Way
  • Constructing synthetic Gaia DR3 surveys from Milky Way-like galaxies in the FIRE simulation

In the first two year of my Ph.D., I work with the MIT LIGO Laboratory to apply convolutional neural network to subtract non-linear noise coupling mechanisms using auxiliary channels in gravitational-wave detectors.
